Let's explore a world of creativity

Thapasya Muthanna K Date: Thu 01, 2025 12

Art, as in "painting-like thing," refers specifically to painting, which is a major branch of the visual arts. Painting is the practice of applying pigments—such as paints, colors, or other media—onto a solid surface (traditionally called the support) like canvas, paper, wood, or even walls. The result is a two-dimensional artwork known as a painting.

A painting uses fundamental visual elements such as color, line, shape, texture, and tone to convey aesthetic quality, tell stories, express emotions, or present ideas. Traditional media include oil paint, watercolor, acrylic, tempera, gouache, and more, each offering distinct effects and techniques. Paintings can be highly representational (realistic), abstract, or even a blend of both

Over time, the definition has broadened: while classical paintings were mostly on canvas or panel, today, surfaces can vary widely (walls, glass, even digital screens), and new materials or approaches (like mixed media) continue to challenge the boundaries of what painting is.
